Overview

Flood management is the process of reducing the risk and impact of floods on people and the environment. It involves a range of activities, including prevention, response, and recovery.

Prevention

Preventing floods involves a range of measures, including:

  • Building flood defences, such as levees and flood walls
  • Managing land use to reduce the risk of flooding
  • Improving drainage systems
  • Developing early warning systems

Response

Responding to floods involves a range of activities, including:

  • Evacuating people from affected areas
  • Providing emergency shelter and supplies
  • Rescuing people who are trapped or in danger
  • Assessing damage and coordinating recovery efforts

Recovery

Recovering from floods involves a range of activities, including:

  • Cleaning up and repairing damage
  • Providing support to affected communities
  • Rebuilding infrastructure and homes
  • Developing long-term flood management plans
